Mt. Fuji 100: Where to Stay Before and After the Race — A Practical Guide for Runners and Their Families

Checked

Written for: Runners and companions planning lodging and logistics around the Mt. Fuji 100 race weekend.

The official Mt.FUJI100 English site identifies Fuji Hokuroku Park as the main venue and names two event accommodation options: PICA Fujiyoshida and Mt. Fuji Resort Hotel. For the 2026 event, the related packages went on sale on December 3 in Japan and December 10 for overseas customers. The 2027 race dates, accommodation packages, and transport arrangements have not yet been announced, so confirm the latest official information before booking.

Source-checked Japan verified on: 2026-07-19 — leading fact: Fuji Hokuroku Park is the officially named main venue, and the official English site names exactly two event accommodation options. Confirmed

Why this exists

The official English race site provides a useful starting point, including two named accommodation options and event transport information. It does not provide an independent comparison or a detailed plan for accompanying family members. This guide therefore focuses on separating confirmed race information from arrangements that travelers must verify after the 2027 schedule is announced.

No reproducible claim is made about the number of English search results or the absence of independent guides.

What we verified

Every item below was checked against a primary source. Each fact carries its confirmed date and source. Anything we could not confirm is kept separately under "What we could not verify."

Scope of lodging/tour guidance on the official English race site, and what an independent comparison + companion guide still needs to add Confirmed

Source: Official race site

Confirmed 2026-07-19 (en.mtfuji100.com/access/tour/): PICA Fujiyoshida and Mt. Fuji Resort Hotel remain the only two properties named directly by the official English site, each with event-specific pre/post-race packages by distance category, on sale from 12:00 noon JST Dec 3 (overseas from Dec 10). The page is labeled "Mt.FUJI 100 2026" with no 2027 version yet. This confirms the article's premise: official guidance exists but is narrow (two named properties, no independent comparison, no companion-specific plan) — the gap is real but narrower than "no official guidance at all."

Existence of additional Kawaguchiko / Fujiyoshida properties that maintain their own official English-language pages Confirmed

Source: Each property's own official site

Confirmed 2026-07-19 via each property's own official site: West Inn Fuji-Yoshida (westinn.jp/en/), Kawaguchiko Lakeside Hotel (lakeside.co.jp/), and Highland Resort Hotel & Spa (fujiq.jp/en/relate/highlandresort.html) each maintain an English-language official page. This record confirms only that these properties exist and publish information in English. No price, availability, or booking-inventory claim is made — see "What we could not verify."

Access to the start/finish venue (Fuji Hokuroku Park) and whether an English shuttle guide exists Confirmed

Source: Official race site

Confirmed 2026-07-19: en.mtfuji100.com/access/access/ has an English section on the race circulation shuttle ("Shuttle buses will be operating between the main venue (Fuji Hokuroku Park) and each tournament's parking lot..."), fares ¥3,300 (3-day), ¥2,200 (2-day), ¥1,700 (1-day), running 4/24–26 roughly every 30–60 min. Fuji Hokuroku Park itself is confirmed via its own official site (hokuroku.jp).

Existence and operating details of individual companion-friendly attractions near the race area (Lake Kawaguchiko cruise, Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway, Oshino Hakkai) Confirmed

Source: Each attraction's own operator site

Confirmed 2026-07-19 via each operator's own site: the Lake Kawaguchiko sightseeing cruise (fujigokokisen.jp, ¥1,000, about 20 minutes, departures roughly every 30 minutes), Oshino Hakkai (oshino-navi.com/187-2, free to enter except Sokonashi-ike at ¥300), and the Mt. Fuji Panoramic (Tenjozan) Ropeway (mtfujiropeway.jp), which lists 2026 maintenance closures of 5/11–7/15 and 12/7–12/18; as of this check the 5/11–7/15 window had ended and normal operation (8:30–17:00) had resumed. This record covers the individual attractions only. No bundled tour product, third-party price, or booking availability is claimed — see "What we could not verify."

2027 race dates and entry period Confirmed

Source: Official race site

Confirmed 2026-07-19: no 2027 date or entry-period announcement exists on mtfuji100.com/news/, en.mtfuji100.com, or the UTMB World Series calendar (which does not list Mt.FUJI100 at all, since the race left the UTMB series in 2023 and has run under World Trail Majors since 2024). The article's hedge — "2027 dates have not been announced as of {date}" — is confirmed accurate as written.

What we could NOT verify

The following could not be verified from primary or official sources during the 2026-07-19 review: live room availability or race-week prices for any property; whether accommodation will become scarce after the 2027 dates are announced; the third-party booking-site prices previously quoted in this article; the existence, price, duration, availability, or cancellation terms of any bundled companion tour sold through third-party booking platforms; and the previously quoted limited-express train frequency, journey time, and fare. The review also did not reproduce the earlier claim of approximately 28 search results and no independent guides. Finally, the 2027 race dates, entry period, official accommodation packages, package sale schedule, and shuttle timetable have not yet been announced. Any 2026 details in this article are historical reference points, not promises for 2027.

How to actually go

Use the official race information as the starting point, then confirm 2027 accommodation and transport directly after the new event schedule is announced.

  • Official accommodation baseline: the Mt.FUJI100 English site names PICA Fujiyoshida and Mt. Fuji Resort Hotel. For the 2026 event, its pre- and post-race packages went on sale at 12:00 noon JST on December 3, with overseas sales beginning at 18:00 JST on December 10. The equivalent 2027 packages and sale dates have not been announced.
  • Other properties with confirmed English-language official pages include West Inn Fuji-Yoshida, Kawaguchiko Lakeside Hotel, and Highland Resort Hotel & Spa. This review did not verify their race-week prices or availability, so check directly with each property.
  • Venue: the official race site identifies Fuji Hokuroku Park as the main venue.
  • Shuttle reference: for the 2026 event, the official race circulation shuttle connected Fujisan Station, Fuji-Q Highland, and Fuji Hokuroku Park. Passes cost ¥3,300 for three days, ¥2,200 for two days, and ¥1,700 for one day. These fares and routes are confirmed only for the 2026 edition; the 2027 service has not been announced.
  • Companion options confirmed through their official operators include the Lake Kawaguchiko sightseeing cruise, the Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway, and Oshino Hakkai. No bundled tour, third-party price, live availability, or cancellation policy was verified.

The official ropeway operator listed maintenance closures from May 11 to July 15 and from December 7 to December 18 in 2026. Those dates should not be assumed to apply in 2027. Check the operator's current notice and the official race schedule before building a companion itinerary.
