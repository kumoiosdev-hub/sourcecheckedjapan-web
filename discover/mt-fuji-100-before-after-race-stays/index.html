Mt. Fuji 100 (UTMF): Where to Stay Before and After the Race — A Practical Guide for Runners and Their Families

Verification pending

Written for: Runners and companions planning lodging and logistics around the Mt. Fuji 100 (UTMF) race weekend.

The official Mt. Fuji 100 English site lists a small number of lodging and tour options, but it does not replace an independent comparison or a companion-first plan. The practical choice is whether to stay closer to the venue or closer to the wider Fuji Five Lakes visitor area.

Source-checked Japan verified on: not yet completed — leading fact: Race-week lodging inventory and shuttle guidance is the leading fact to verify before publication. Verification pending

Why this exists

An initial review of roughly 28 English search results did not find an independent guide that combines lodging comparison with a practical plan for companions. The official English site does list limited accommodation and tour options, so the gap is narrower than first assumed. How to actually go

Verification pending

Base options: Kawaguchiko (larger tourist/family inventory) or Fujiyoshida (closer to the start venue).

  • Candidate lodging (AI-collected, individual pages not yet human-confirmed): West Inn Fuji-Yoshida (~$85–92 observed, KAYAK low $57), Kawaguchiko Lakeside Hotel ($146+), Fuji Kawaguchiko Resort Hotel, Highland Resort Hotel & Spa. Booking.com individual pages returned bot-blocked (403) responses on two consecutive collection attempts — a human browser session is required to confirm.
  • Two properties named directly by the official race site (kept here as the comparison baseline): PICA Fujiyoshida (about 7 minutes on foot from the finish; official check-in 14:00–19:00 / check-out 8:00–11:00, confirmed 2026-07-19 via pica-resort.jp/en/yoshida/about/guidance.html) and Mt. Fuji Resort Hotel.
  • From Tokyo: highway bus from Shinjuku, roughly 1–2 departures/hour, about 1h45m, ¥2,000–2,200; or the JR limited express "Fuji Excursion," 4 round trips/day, about 114 minutes, ¥4,200.
  • Venue access: the official race shuttle (Fujisan Station – Fuji-Q Highland – venue) ran 4/24–26 in the 2026 edition, roughly every 30–60 minutes, ¥3,300 for a 3-day pass, with English guidance. Note: the FUJI100mi start (Kodomo no Kuni) uses a separate shuttle.
  • Companion time: a Kawaguchiko cruise + ropeway + Oshino Hakkai English tour currently exists on both Viator and GetYourGuide (around $52/person, ~10h, free cancellation 24h ahead).

Mt. Fuji Tenjozan Ropeway is scheduled for maintenance closure 5/11–7/15 and 12/7–12/18 in 2026 (replaced by a sightseeing boat during closure). As of the 2026-07-19 verification check, the 5/11–7/15 window has already ended and the ropeway is back to normal operation; only the 12/7–12/18 window is still upcoming. This does not appear to overlap the usual late-April race window, but 2027 dates are unconfirmed, so it stays in the article as Plan B material.
