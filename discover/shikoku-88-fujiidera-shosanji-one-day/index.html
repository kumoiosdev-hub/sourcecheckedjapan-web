Walking One Day of the Shikoku 88 Pilgrimage: The Fujiidera-to-Shosanji "Henro Korogashi" Section

Verification pending

Written for: Travelers who cannot walk the full Shikoku 88 circuit but want one demanding, authentic day-section with a realistic return plan.

You do not need to walk the full Shikoku pilgrimage to experience one demanding section, but the Fujiidera-to-Shosanji route is not a turn-up-and-go day trip. Reaching the trailhead may be manageable from Tokushima; getting back from Shosanji is the harder problem and may require a pre-booked taxi.

Source-checked Japan verified on: not yet completed — leading fact: The current return-trip arrangement from Shosanji is the leading fact to verify before publication. Verification pending

Why this exists

Many English guides are designed for people walking a much larger part of the circuit. The one-day visitor has a different job: confirm the trailhead approach, arrange the return before starting, set a turnaround rule, and carry only what this section requires. This article should solve that logistics problem rather than claim the route is easy to combine with public transport. Verification pending

Distance, elevation gain, and time-required figures vary across sources and will be shown as a range only after human confirmation.

Public transport to Fujiidera (Temple 11) — JR Tokushima Line, Kamojima Station, then on foot/taxi Verification pending

Source: Timetable data (to be measured directly)

Verified 2026-07-19: Tokushima Station → Kamojima Station is confirmed via JR Shikoku's official timetable PDFs (jr-shikoku.co.jp, 2025.03.15 revision). Earliest departure toward Awa-Ikeda/Anabuki is 06:23 (limited express "Tsurugisan 1" — requires a separate limited-express ticket; Kamojima is a stop), earliest local (non-reserved) train departs 06:54. Kamojima → Fujiidera on foot/by taxi still has no primary-source distance/time figure — the Shikoku Pilgrimage Association's official Fujiidera page lists only address, phone, and parking, no access detail — that leg remains unconfirmed.

Section distance / elevation gain / standard time (12.3km / +600m vs. +1,100m — needs a primary-source resolution) Verification pending

Source: Geospatial Information Authority of Japan / official pilgrimage materials

Checked 2026-07-19: distance remains unresolved between two primary sources — Tokushima Prefecture's official "Shikoku no Michi" page states 15.6km, while summing the Henro-michi Hozon Kyoryokukai's official section breakdown (Fujiidera→Nagatoan 3.2km→Ryusuian 3.4km→Jorenan 2.2km→Shosanji 4.1km) gives 12.9km — a roughly 2.7km gap with no documented explanation. The article's range framing should be kept rather than a single number. Elevation gain remains unconfirmed — no static primary-source page states it; only GSI's interactive map tool could resolve it, requiring manual human use. Standard time is CONFIRMED at approximately 7 hours 30 minutes (Tokushima Prefecture official page, pref.tokushima.lg.jp/shikoku-nature-trail/course/course09/). The oft-cited trailhead-sign wording ("5h fast / 6h average / 8h slow") could not be confirmed on any official page and should be attributed as "per the on-site trail sign" if used.

Current descent option from Shosanji (Temple 12): the old scheduled bus is reported discontinued; what alternative exists for visitors, taxi operators, booking conditions, approximate fare Verification pending

Source: Official sources + direct confirmation

Verified 2026-07-19: the Kamiyama Town scheduled bus line to Shosanji is CONFIRMED discontinued effective 2023-03-31 (town.kamiyama.lg.jp/office/juumin/general-affairs/bus.html, still current as of its 2025-10-01 update — no replacement route). The town's on-demand alternative, "Machi no Kuruma Let's," is CONFIRMED restricted to registered Kamiyama Town residents (My Number Card registration required) — pilgrims/visitors are not eligible. Three taxi operators checked: Tsubame Kanko is CONFIRMED via its official site (operates a reservation-only "Shosanji sanpai bus," 20+ people, Nabeiwa parking ⇄ Shosanji ~5km/~20min, ¥1,500/person, ¥2,000/vehicle parking, tel. 088-678-0224, 8:00–17:00); Yoi Kanko and Kawamata Taxi were confirmed to exist via directory listings (address/phone) but not yet cross-checked against Google Maps directly. Individual (non-group) pilgrim taxi booking availability and fare remain unconfirmed — still pending the phone-check sheet (Tier2).

Current operating status and booking channel of nearby lodging (Sudachian, HENRO HOUSE Cotton Field, Awarakuya, etc.) — English availability is not claimed and must be confirmed per property Verification pending

Source: Direct confirmation, per property

Verified 2026-07-19. Sudachian: CONFIRMED via official site (sudachian.com) — ¥7,900 (2 meals), ¥6,900 (breakfast only), ¥5,900 (dinner only), ¥4,900 (room only), phone reservation 090-2677-8000; English support is not stated on the page and remains unconfirmed. HENRO HOUSE Cotton Field: CONFIRMED via official site (henrohouse.jp/en/houses/79) — ¥4,400 room-only, cash only, max 4 guests/1 group per night, online booking with an advance-day cutoff, footer explicitly lists "Languages: English | 日本語." Awarakuya: location CONFIRMED and CORRECTED — the official site (wwwe.pikara.ne.jp/awarakuya/access.html) gives the address as Kawajima-cho, Yoshinogawa City (〒779-3301), between Temple 10 (Kirihataji) and Temple 11 (Fujiidera), 7 minutes on foot from JR Awa-Kawajima Station — i.e. Yoshinogawa City, not Kamiyama Town. It should be positioned as a pre-Fujiidera staging option, not grouped with Kamiyama-side (post-Shosanji) lodging.

How to actually go

Verification pending

Pre-night base: Tokushima City (larger lodging inventory) or around Kamojima (closer to Fujiidera; Awarakuya reportedly offers pickup — to verify).

  • Morning approach: Tokushima Station → Kamojima Station (JR Tokushima Line) → Fujiidera. Timing and first departure to verify.
  • After the descent: Shosanji → Kamiyama Town → Tokushima City. The old scheduled bus is reported discontinued — arranging the return in advance is the central logistics problem of this article. The 2026 existence, booking conditions, and approximate fare of a taxi option must be human-confirmed before this is stated as fact.
