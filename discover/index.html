Walking One Day of the Shikoku 88 Pilgrimage: The Fujiidera-to-Shosanji "Henro Korogashi" Section

Verification pending

You do not need to walk the full Shikoku pilgrimage to experience one demanding section, but the Fujiidera-to-Shosanji route is not a turn-up-and-go day trip. Reaching the trailhead may be manageable from Tokushima; getting back from Shosanji is the harder problem and may require a pre-booked taxi.

Soy Sauce Pilgrimage in Yuasa, Wakayama: The Town Where Japanese Shoyu Began

Verification pending

Yuasa, a small port town in Wakayama Prefecture, is where Japanese soy sauce brewing is said to have originated in the 13th century. You can walk its preserved merchant district, visit working breweries that still ferment in cedar barrels, and do it as a half-day stop between Koyasan and Shirahama.
